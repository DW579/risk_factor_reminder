Conditions/Diseases

Macular Degeneration, AMD

Age-related Macular Degeneration (AMD) is an age-associated degenerative disease of the macula, the central part of the retina (the sensory tissue that lines the back of the eye) that allows the eye to see fine details. AMD is a leading cause of vision loss among people age 50 and older.

Alzheimer Disease

Alzheimer's Disease is a degenerative disease that slowly destroys brain cells. The symptoms of Alzheimer's disease get worse over time, but the progression of the disease varies. In the early stages it manifests as memory loss and as it progresses results in loss of thinking and language skills (cognitive ability) as well as behavioral changes.

Coronary Artery Disease

Coronary artery disease is a blockage or damage of the arteries that supply blood, oxygen and nutrients to the heart muscle, often due to a buildup of plaque inside the arteries.
